Run the room

The activity is the easy part. These four panels are what keeps forty kids inside a classroom from turning into forty kids inside a classroom.

Attention-getters

Teach one on day one and use only that one. Switching signals is why signals stop working.

  • "Hands on top…" → group answers "…that means stop!" hands on head.
  • "Macaroni and cheese" → "Everybody freeze!"
  • Clap pattern — you clap a rhythm, they echo it. Make each one harder.
  • Countdown from 5 with fingers up, silent. Slowest signal, best for testing days.
  • "Give me five" — eyes, ears, mouth, hands, body. Point to each.
  • Lights off, count to 3 — for a room that's already loud.

Indoor ground rules

Say these out loud before the first activity, every single indoor day.

  • Walking feet only. Tile plus sneakers equals a nurse visit.
  • Furniture stays put unless you moved it and you'll move it back.
  • Nothing thrown above shoulder height — soft items only, no hard balls indoors.
  • Clear sightlines. Every child stays where you can see them; no one behind a bookshelf or in a closet.
  • Voice level 2. Name it, model it, reset it when it climbs.
  • Leave it better. Two-minute reset before dismissal, non-negotiable.

Indoor bin checklist

Keep one stocked bin per group. Restock Fridays so Monday's storm isn't a scramble.

  • Deck of cards ×2, dice, dominoes
  • Plastic cups (50+), painter's tape, yarn or string
  • Balloons, beanbags or rolled socks, foam ball
  • Blank paper, construction paper, index cards
  • Markers, crayons, pencils, safety scissors, glue sticks
  • Whiteboard markers + eraser (check with the classroom teacher first)
  • Timer or phone stopwatch, small speaker
  • Two or three read-aloud chapter books

Pick the right activity

Match the activity to the room, not to your favorites list.

  • Just came inside, high energy? Start with Move It, then step down.
  • Homework just ended? Circle Up — social, seated, low friction.
  • Teacher's room, delicate stuff out? Think It or Make It at tables only.
  • Last 20 minutes before pickup? Wind Down, so kids leave regulated.
  • Split-age group? Pair a 4th/5th grader with a K/1 as a "coach."
  • Someone sitting out? Give them a job — scorekeeper, timer, judge.

Three-minute rule

  1. Explain in under a minute. If it takes longer, demo it instead of describing it.
  2. Play one practice round where nobody's out and nothing counts.
  3. If it isn't working three minutes in, change one rule out loud — don't abandon it. Kids read a scrapped game as your failure, and a tweaked rule as their win.
