Run the field

Outside, the risks change. Heat, sightlines, storms, and equipment that walks off. These four panels are the part of the job that isn't the game.

Heat, water, and shade

August through October in Broward, the heat index is the real supervisor. Plan around it before you plan the game.

  • Water break every 20 minutes, called by you — not when kids ask. Thirst shows up late.
  • Heat index above 103° — shade or indoor only, no sustained running games.
  • Rotate through shade, don't camp in the sun. Split the group and swap halfway.
  • Watch for the quiet one. A kid who stops complaining and sits down is the one to check on.
  • Headache, nausea, clammy skin, no sweat — get them inside and get the front office now.
  • You drink too. A dehydrated counselor makes bad calls at the worst moment.

Boundaries and head counts

Say the boundaries out loud and point to them every time, even with the same group you had yesterday.

  • Count at the door, at the field, and before you come back in. Three counts, every time.
  • Stand on the edge, not the middle. Position yourself so the whole group is in one field of view.
  • Two counselors, opposite corners. Never both on the same side of the play area.
  • Name the boundary with an object — the fence, the third cone, the sidewalk crack. Not "over there."
  • Bathroom runs go in pairs and you note the time they left.
  • Scan the perimeter every few minutes — gates, parking lot side, anyone approaching the fence.

Equipment check-out

Reserve it on the sheet before your shift. Anything not on the sheet may already be spoken for by another group.

  • Reserve ahead on the equipment reservation workbook — initials in the box for your week.
  • Count out, count in. Say the number out loud to a second counselor at both ends.
  • Assign a gear captain from the group. Kids return equipment far better than they receive requests to.
  • Balls get checked for pressure and hoops for cracks before they go out, not after.
  • Report damage the same day — a split gaga ball or a bent cornhole leg comes back to the supervisor, not the shed.
  • Nothing goes home. Speed Stacks cups and Velcro balls are the ones that vanish.

Weather calls

South Florida afternoon storms build fast. The call is yours to make early, not the sky's to make for you.

  • Thunder means done. Any thunder, any distance — everyone inside immediately.
  • 30 minutes clear after the last thunder before you go back out. Restart the clock each time.
  • Watch the western sky around 3 p.m. Dark base plus rising wind means pack up now.
  • Have the indoor plan ready before you walk out — pick a backup from the indoor guide first.
  • Wet grass after rain — no running games. Switch to Target & Toss on the pavement.
  • Fire ant mounds get flagged with a cone and reported. Walk the field before the group does.

The two-minute walk-out

  1. Head count and boundaries before anyone touches a ball. Point at the actual boundary.
  2. Sweep the play area — glass, ant mounds, sprinkler heads, anything left from the school day.
  3. State the game, the boundary, and the water-break signal in one breath. Then start.
  4. Last five minutes are for equipment round-up and the final count, not one more round. Budget it.
